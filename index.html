1) Maps presence is the leading indicator across the cohort. Every account gaining cells is gaining intake. The accounts where Maps is plateauing (Surfpoint, partial Arms Acres) are bottlenecked by structural issues — not by lack of demand.

2) Site structure is the next leg, uniformly. Every account has high-intent service-page and location-page queries already ranking on page 1 that need on-site work to convert. This is where Q3 hours compound.

3) Brand search is healthy across all 6. The treatment programs are reputable and earning organic demand. The lever isn't brand — it's category capture.

4) Off-page program halt (2026-05-13) is now applied across the cohort. SEO-NEO / DAS / Blast / Sniper / RD100 / NAP-overlay / AI Articles / Driving Directions / Cloud Page / Embedinator / Link Revolver / BatchGeo / ATB / GMB-Pin tasks are off the rotations on all 6 accounts. Replacing with manual citations + digital PR + partner outreach + on-site link equity. Recovery cohort now has the cleanest off-page profile in the book.

5) Saturation is real and accelerating. Every market we track is more crowded than Q2 last year. AI Overviews are now triggering on treatment queries that previously had clean blue-link SERPs. The cohort is winning on substance — clear service pages, real GBP presence, owned brand — which is exactly what the new SERP architecture rewards.

Q3 FOCUS, RANKED BY EXPECTED IMPACT

1. Arms Acres — secure manager access on the 3 unmanaged GBPs. Highest-leverage single action across the cohort.
